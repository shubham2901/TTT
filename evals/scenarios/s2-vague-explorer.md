# S2: Vague Explorer

**Tests:** Thesis narrowing, proportionate questioning, assumption-making, user patience
**Tonal register:** Rambling, uncertain, responsive to guidance

---

## Default Scenario

### Opening (Turn 1)

**User:** "Something with AI for creators, not sure what exactly. Maybe helping them be more productive? I don't know, there are so many tools already."

### Turn 2

User responds with partial information, rambling tone:

**User:** "I guess I'm thinking about YouTube creators mostly? Like they spend so much time editing and promoting. I've seen some AI tools but they all feel generic. I don't know if I should do editing or promotion or something else. What do you think?"

### Turn 3

User responds to TTT's narrowed direction:

**User:** "Yeah that sounds interesting. Let's go with that. I'm building this solo, probably a web app."

### After Scripted Turns

Evaluator continues as a responsive-but-uncertain user. Defers to TTT's recommendations when unsure. Answers questions but doesn't volunteer extra info. Documents each TTT narrowing attempt in transcript.

---

## Key Checkpoints

### 1. After Turn 1 — Initial Narrowing Attempt

TTT should NOT immediately fire 5 questions at a vague user. Should make an initial narrowing attempt — suggest 2–3 possible directions or ask ONE focusing question. Must acknowledge the user's uncertainty without dismissing it. Choreographer question discipline: max 2 questions per message.

### 2. After Turn 2 — Measurable Thesis Narrowing

Thesis should have narrowed measurably from the opening. TTT should have formed an opinionated stance about direction and presented it with reasoning — not just reflected "what do you want?" back at the user. Should reference the user's mention of "generic" tools as a whitespace signal for differentiation.

### 3. After Clarify Phase Completes

`clarification.md` thesis should be substantially more specific than the opening (from "something with AI for creators" → one-sentence thesis about a specific product). Assumptions should be documented with confidence levels. Specificity score ≥ 6. Assumptions section should be longer than in S1 — more gaps were filled by TTT's judgment.

---

## Success Definition

### Qualitative

- Thesis narrows measurably across turns (from 5+ possible products → 1 specific thesis)
- TTT takes opinionated stances with reasoning, not just reflecting questions back
- User is not bombarded with questions — max 2 per message observed
- Assumptions are stated explicitly, not baked in silently
- TTT doesn't dismiss the user's vagueness or force premature specificity

### Rubric Expectations

| Dimension         | Expected | Rationale                                                |
|-------------------|----------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| coherence         | 3–4      | Vague input → assumptions needed → minor tensions likely |
| specificity       | 3–4      | Started vague — TTT must narrow, unlikely to reach 5     |
| relevance         | 4–5      | Research should match the narrowed thesis                 |
| comprehensiveness | 4–5      | All sections should still be filled                      |
| actionability     | 3–4      | Assumptions may reduce downstream confidence             |

## Variants

### Variant A: Vague B2B Idea

**Opening:** "I want to build something for small businesses. Like, they struggle with so much stuff. Maybe invoicing? Or scheduling? Not sure."

**Turn 2:** "Hmm, I was thinking local service businesses actually. Like plumbers, electricians. They lose jobs because they take too long to reply to inquiries. Does that help?"

**Turn 3:** "OK let's do that. Web app I think. Building solo."

**Notes:** Tests narrowing from extremely broad ("small businesses") to specific segment ("local service businesses losing jobs to slow response"). TTT should latch onto the pain signal in Turn 2 and form a thesis around response speed.

### Variant B: Vague Personal Project

**Opening:** "I want to make my own app but I don't really have a specific idea. I like cooking and I think there should be something better than existing recipe apps. They all suck but I can't explain why."

**Turn 2:** "I think it's that they assume you know how to cook already? Like, I'm a beginner and the instructions are always unclear. Also I hate meal planning."

**Turn 3:** "Sure, that direction works. Mobile app makes sense for cooking."

**Notes:** Tests TTT with a feeling-driven user who can't articulate the problem. TTT should extract pain points ("unclear instructions," "beginner-unfriendly," "hate meal planning") from vague complaints and form a thesis. Platform should resolve to mobile based on cooking context.

---

Each variant preserves scenario intent — a vague, uncertain user who needs TTT to narrow the thesis — but forces different narrowing paths and domain reasoning.
